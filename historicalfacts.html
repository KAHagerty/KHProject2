Mental health in the past was handled in ways that are now
far from considerable as humane by today's standards. A common
treatment for mental disorders was a lobotomy. A lobotomy was
the practice of inserting a very long needle into the prefrontal
lobe of the brain, which they accessed under the eyelid.
Another treatment was called trepanning. Mental issues were
thought to be due to demons entering the body, and trepanation was
the practice of sawing a large hole in the skull in order to
"let the demons out".
